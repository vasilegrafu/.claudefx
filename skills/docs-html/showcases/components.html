Category 01 / 12

Structure (structure)

The document skeleton — cover, contents, sections and prose that give every document its shape.

Lead paragraph

lead

The executive-summary opener: one emphasised paragraph stating what this document establishes and why it matters, before any detail.

Section & subsection

section

A section heading

Sections carry an anchored, optionally-numbered heading and wrap a block of the document.

A subsection

Subsections nest one level deeper for finer structure.

Prose paragraph

prose

The body text wrapper — measured line length, inline code, links and definitions. Every readable paragraph on this page is prose.

Also in this category and used to build the page itself: metadata-header (the cover), toc (the contents block above), and page — which renders only inside <body class="presentation">, see the presentation doc-type.

Thesis pillars — claim, evidence, falsifier

thesis-pillars

  1. Services compounds at 12%+ through FY2028

    Services grew 13.1% in FY2025 to $109.2B on a 74% gross margin; installed base up 7% y/y.

    Breaks if Two consecutive quarters of Services growth below 8%, or an adverse EU ruling on App Store terms.

  2. Gross margin re-rates the whole business

    Mix shift lifted group gross margin from 43.3% to 46.9% over three years.

    Breaks if Group gross margin falls below 44% for two consecutive quarters.

  3. Buybacks shrink the share count 3% a year

    $95B repurchased in FY2025 against a $3.4T cap; net cash still above $50B.

    Breaks if Net buyback drops below $60B a year, or net cash turns negative.

Notes cited in the statements above

Note 2 Revenue recognition

Services revenue is recognised over the contract term. Deferred revenue of $9.1B at year end is disclosed separately and is not in the Services line above.

Note 5 Income taxes watch

The FY2024 provision includes a one-off $10.2B charge from a State Aid decision. The FY2025 effective rate of 17.0% is the run rate; FY2024 net income is not comparable without adjusting for it.

Note 8 Share-based compensation watch

$12.6B was expensed in FY2025 and added back in operating cash flow. Dilution is offset by buybacks, so the cost is real but never appears in the share count.

Note 12 Debt and commitments

Term debt of $82.3B carries a weighted average rate of 3.1%, with a maturity wall of $18.4B in FY2027. Operating lease commitments of $14.2B are not included in term debt.

Five forces — industry structure

five-forces

Smartphone and platform industry structure

Threat of new entrants Low

Silicon design, a two-million-app ecosystem and $30B+ of annual R&D make entry uneconomic; no new entrant has taken 1% share in a decade.

Supplier power Low

Displays and memory are commoditised across three vendors each; in-house silicon removed the largest supplier dependency.

Buyer power Moderate

Consumers are price-sensitive at the low end but switching costs are high once inside the ecosystem; carrier subsidies have largely gone.

Threat of substitutes Low

No category substitutes for the smartphone; wearables extend the platform rather than replacing it.

Competitive rivalry Moderate

Android competes hard on price and specification but not on ecosystem lock-in; the premium tier is effectively a duopoly.

Structurally attractive for the incumbent: the binding constraint is regulation, which sits outside Porter’s frame entirely.
