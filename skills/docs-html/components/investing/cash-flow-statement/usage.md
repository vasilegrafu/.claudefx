# cash-flow-statement

_Authoring guidance for the `cash-flow-statement` component — when to use it, how, and the rules._

Styled by: `css/modules/investing.css`

The three cash sections across periods, plus the derivation that the statement
itself never prints: free cash flow, written out as arithmetic. The `fcf`
string is the component's reason to exist — every reader computes it, so the
document should state which definition it used rather than leave five plausible
answers open.

```jinja
{{ c.cash_flow_statement(
    caption="Consolidated statements of cash flows", unit="$M",
    source="FY2025 Form 10-K, page 35.",
    periods=["FY2023", "FY2024", "FY2025"],
    fcf="Operating cash $128,900M − capital expenditure $12,100M = $116,800M, 28.1% of revenue and 105% of net income.",
    rows=[
        ("Operating activities",                  [],                                     "section",  ""),
        ("Net income",                            ["96,995", "93,736", "111,600"],        "",         ""),
        ("Depreciation and amortisation",         ["11,519", "11,445", "12,200"],         "detail",   ""),
        ("Share-based compensation",              ["10,833", "11,688", "12,600"],         "detail",   "8"),
        ("Changes in working capital",            ["(6,577)", "(6,577)", "(4,100)"],      "detail",   ""),
        ("Cash generated by operating activities", ["110,543", "118,254", "128,900"],     "subtotal", ""),
        ("Investing activities",                  [],                                     "section",  ""),
        ("Payments for property, plant and equipment", ["(10,959)", "(9,447)", "(12,100)"], "detail", ""),
        ("Cash generated by/(used in) investing",  ["3,705", "2,935", "(4,200)"],         "subtotal", ""),
        ("Financing activities",                  [],                                     "section",  ""),
        ("Dividends paid",                        ["(15,025)", "(15,234)", "(15,900)"],   "detail",   ""),
        ("Repurchases of common stock",           ["(77,550)", "(94,949)", "(95,000)"],   "detail",   ""),
        ("Cash used in financing activities",     ["(108,488)", "(121,983)", "(121,600)"], "subtotal", ""),
        ("Increase/(decrease) in cash",           ["5,760", "(794)", "3,100"],            "total",    ""),
    ]) }}
```

Rules: the `fcf` line states its DEFINITION, not just its result — operating
cash less capital expenditure is the default, and any other treatment (leases,
acquisitions, capitalised software) is named explicitly. Express free cash flow
against something: percent of revenue, percent of net income, or per share.
Share-based compensation is a real cost added back in operating cash — show the
line and cite its note rather than letting it disappear into "adjustments".
Cash outflows are negative and in parentheses everywhere, including buybacks
and dividends. Where operating cash and net income diverge for more than one
period, that divergence is the finding — say so under the table, not in a
caption.
